Semiotic truth

Main ideas:

Structuralist criticism:

  • Culmination of the development of text-oriented theories in the twentieth century
  • Founded on Ferdinand de Saussure’s theory of parole and langue
  • Ontic truth is to be found not in the matrix of a particular text ( parole,) but in a semiotic system (langue) governing all texts
  • The cognition of this ontic truth entails an investigation of intersubjective conventions of signification that govern the functioning of all texts
  • Departure from Romantic and New Critical emphasis on “particularity” and “subjectivity” and the structuralist return to Aristotelian concerns with the universal, operative truth.
  • Endeavor to unveil the universal truth through an analysis of the deep, global system of signifying conventions that makes possible language and literature as well as all other cultural phenomena
